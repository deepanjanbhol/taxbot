# The "One Big Beautiful Bill" — Tax Provisions Reference

> **IMPORTANT:** This knowledge file is based on provisions as of mid-2025. The bill passed the
> U.S. House of Representatives in May 2025. Verify current enacted law with the IRS or a CPA
> before filing. Provisions may have changed in Senate amendments or implementation rules.

---

## What Is It?

The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (H.R. 1, 119th Congress) is a large budget reconciliation
bill that, among many provisions, permanently extends and expands the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act
(TCJA) of 2017 — which was otherwise set to expire after 2025.

---

## Key Individual Tax Provisions

### 1. Permanent TCJA Individual Tax Rates

The lower rates enacted in 2017 (10/12/22/24/32/35/37%) are made permanent.
Without the bill, rates would have reverted to pre-2017 levels (10/15/25/28/33/35/39.6%).

### 2. Increased Standard Deduction (permanent)

| Filing Status | TCJA 2025 | Under Big Beautiful Bill |
|---------------|-----------|--------------------------|
| Single | $15,000 | ~$16,000+ (indexed) |
| Married Filing Jointly | $30,000 | ~$32,000+ (indexed) |
| Head of Household | $22,500 | ~$24,000+ (indexed) |

### 3. No Tax on Tips ("Tips Deduction")

- Qualified workers earning under $150,000 can exclude tip income from gross income
- Cap: $25,000 in tip income excluded per year
- Applies to industries where tipping is customary (food service, hospitality, etc.)
- **Document needed:** Track tip income separately on W-2 or own records
- Not available for managers, supervisors, or owners

### 4. No Tax on Overtime Pay

- Overtime wages excluded from gross income (for hours exceeding 40/week)
- Cap: ~$12,500 per year in excluded overtime income
- Phase-out: begins above $150,000 in income
- **Document needed:** W-2 should break out overtime, or use pay stubs

### 5. New Senior Deduction

- Additional above-the-line deduction of **$4,000** for taxpayers age 65 or older
- Phase-out: $75,000 (single) / $150,000 (MFJ)
- Fully phases out $25,000 above threshold
- Stacks with the existing additional standard deduction for seniors

### 6. Car Loan Interest Deduction

- Up to **$10,000** deduction for interest paid on auto loans
- Vehicle must be **assembled in the United States**
- Applies to personal passenger vehicles
- Phase-out: above $100,000 (single) / $200,000 (MFJ)
- **Document needed:** 1098 equivalent from lender, vehicle VIN/origin info

### 7. Child Tax Credit Increase

- Increased from **$2,000 to $2,500** per qualifying child under 17
- Refundable portion increased proportionally
- Phase-out thresholds maintained ($200K single / $400K MFJ)

### 8. SALT Deduction Cap Increase

- State and Local Tax (SALT) deduction cap raised from **$10,000 to $30,000**
- Applies to the combined deduction of state income/sales taxes + property taxes
- Phase-out may apply at higher income levels (verify exact thresholds)
- This significantly benefits taxpayers in high-tax states (CA, NY, NJ, IL, CT, MA)

### 9. Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction Enhancement

- QBI deduction rate increased from **20% to 23%** of qualified business income
- Made permanent (was set to expire after 2025)
- W-2 wage and qualified property limits still apply at higher incomes
- Beneficial for freelancers, S-corp owners, sole proprietors, LLCs

### 10. Estate and Gift Tax

- Exemption permanently increased to approximately **$15 million** per person
- ($30M for married couples with portability)
- Prior law: $13.61M (2024), which would have dropped to ~$7M after 2025

## Business Tax Provisions

### Immediate Expensing (100% Bonus Depreciation)
- Reinstated 100% first-year bonus depreciation for qualified property
- Made permanent
- Applies to new and used property placed in service after a specified date

### R&D Expensing
- Domestic R&D costs can again be expensed immediately (rather than amortized over 5 years)
- This reverses the TCJA rule that went into effect for tax years after 2021

### Section 199A Pass-Through Deduction
- Rate increased to 23% and made permanent (see QBI above)

### Corporate AMT
- 15% Corporate Alternative Minimum Tax (CAMT) provisions adjusted

## What This Means for Common Filer Types

### W-2 Employee (No Side Business)
- Check if you received any tip income → potentially excludable
- Check if you earned overtime → potentially excludable up to $12,500
- Age 65+? → Claim the new $4,000 senior deduction
- High SALT state? → SALT cap now $30,000 may make itemizing more attractive
- Kids under 17? → CTC now $2,500 per child

### Self-Employed / Freelancer
- QBI deduction is 23% (up from 20%) of net qualified business income
- 100% bonus depreciation available for equipment purchased
- R&D expenses may be deductible immediately

### Real Estate Investor
- 100% bonus depreciation reinstated for improvements
- QBI deduction applies to rental income (with limitations)
- Passive activity rules still apply

### High-Income Taxpayer ($200K+)
- Top rate stays 37% (not reverting to 39.6%)
- SALT cap of $30K helps those in high-tax states
- QBI phase-outs still apply at higher incomes

## Filing Impact Checklist

When analyzing a taxpayer under the Big Beautiful Bill, check:
- [ ] Did they receive tip income? → Potentially excludable
- [ ] Did they receive overtime pay? → Potentially excludable up to $12,500
- [ ] Are they 65 or older? → $4,000 additional deduction
- [ ] Did they pay auto loan interest on a US-assembled vehicle? → Up to $10,000 deduction
- [ ] Do they have children under 17? → $2,500 CTC per child
- [ ] In a high-tax state? → SALT cap now $30,000
- [ ] Have pass-through/self-employment income? → 23% QBI deduction

## Uncertainty and Caveats

1. Senate may have amended the House-passed bill — exact final thresholds may differ
2. IRS implementation rules and Form 1040 changes may not be finalized until late 2025/early 2026
3. Some provisions may have phase-in dates or retroactive effective dates
4. State tax treatment of these federal changes varies — states may not conform

**Always recommend the taxpayer verify with IRS.gov and a licensed CPA for the final rules.**
