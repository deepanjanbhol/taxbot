# CPA Selection Guide — Finding the Best Tax Professional

## Types of Tax Professionals

| Type | Credential | Can Represent Before IRS? | Best For |
|------|-----------|--------------------------|----------|
| CPA | State-licensed Certified Public Accountant | Yes (full) | Complex returns, small business, audits |
| EA (Enrolled Agent) | IRS-licensed | Yes (full) | All tax matters, often lower cost than CPA |
| Tax Attorney | Bar licensed | Yes (full) | Tax controversy, criminal, estate planning |
| RTRP/AFSP | IRS Annual Filing Season Program | Limited | Simple to moderate returns |
| Unlicensed preparer | None | No | Simple W-2 returns only |
| H&R Block / Jackson Hewitt | Varies | Varies | Straightforward returns |
| TurboTax / DIY | n/a | n/a | Simple returns; save money |

**Recommendation:** For anything involving self-employment, investments, rental property, or business ownership → use a CPA or EA.

## Typical Pricing (2025–2026)

Prices vary widely by complexity and location:

| Return Type | Typical CPA Price Range | EA Price Range |
|-------------|------------------------|----------------|
| Simple W-2 only | $150 – $300 | $100 – $250 |
| W-2 + investments | $200 – $500 | $150 – $400 |
| Schedule C (self-employed) | $400 – $1,200 | $300 – $900 |
| Partnership/S-corp (1065/1120S) | $800 – $3,000 | $600 – $2,000 |
| Complex individual (multiple states, rental, foreign) | $1,000 – $5,000+ | $700 – $3,500 |
| Audit representation | $200–$500/hour | $150–$400/hour |

### Factors That Raise the Price
- Multiple states
- Rental properties (especially multiple)
- International income or FBAR
- Business ownership (S-corp, partnership, sole proprietor)
- Significant investments (cryptocurrency, stock options, RSUs)
- Trust or estate returns
- IRS correspondence or audit
- Late/amended returns

### Ways to Save
1. Organize all documents before the appointment (saves preparer time = your money)
2. Use an EA instead of CPA for tax preparation (same IRS standing, often 20-40% cheaper)
3. Avoid H&R Block/Jackson Hewitt for complex returns (often charge more, less expertise)
4. Ask for a fixed-fee quote upfront, not hourly
5. File early (before March 15) — preparers charge more during peak (March–April)
6. Consider virtual/remote CPA (often 20-30% less than local office)

## How to Evaluate a CPA / EA

### Questions to Ask Before Hiring
1. What is your experience with my type of return (self-employed, rental, international)?
2. Do you provide year-round tax planning or just filing?
3. What is your fee structure — fixed or hourly?
4. Who will actually prepare my return (you, or a junior staff member)?
5. What is your policy if the IRS audits me?
6. How do you stay current with tax law changes?
7. What is your turnaround time?

### Green Flags
- CPA licensed in your state OR EA (verify at IRS.gov/taxpros)
- Member of AICPA or state CPA society
- References/reviews from clients with similar situations
- Transparent fixed-fee pricing
- Uses secure portal for document exchange (not email)
- Proactively asks about life changes (marriage, children, new job, home purchase)
- Reviews prior year return before starting

### Red Flags
- Promises unusually large refunds before seeing your documents
- Charges based on percentage of your refund
- Refuses to sign the return as paid preparer (required by law)
- Won't give you a copy of your return
- Asks you to sign blank forms
- Has disciplinary history (check at IRS.gov/taxpros, state CPA board)

## Where to Search for CPAs

1. **IRS Directory of Federal Tax Return Preparers** — irs.gov/taxpros (verify credentials)
2. **AICPA Find-a-CPA** — aicpa.org
3. **NAEA Find an EA** — naea.org
4. **Yelp / Google Reviews** — search "[city] CPA tax preparation"
5. **LinkedIn** — good for verifying credentials and background
6. **NATP (National Association of Tax Professionals)** — natptax.com
7. **Referral from your attorney, financial advisor, or banker**

## Timing Recommendations

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| January | Gather all documents as they arrive |
| February | Contact preferred CPA — schedule appointment before March |
| By March 15 | Ideal to have appointment for simple-moderate returns |
| March 15 – April 1 | Still achievable for moderate complexity |
| April 1 + | Rush fees likely; consider filing extension (Form 4868) |
| By April 15 | Either file return OR file extension + pay estimated taxes owed |

## Cost–Benefit Analysis

A good CPA/EA typically saves more than they cost by:
- Finding deductions you missed
- Avoiding costly mistakes (penalties, interest)
- Tax planning advice (especially for self-employed)
- Representation if audited

**Rule of thumb:** If your return is simple (W-2 only, standard deduction, no side income) → DIY or H&R Block. If you have complexity → a good CPA/EA pays for itself.
