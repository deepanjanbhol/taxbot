## Social Security Taxation

Up to 85% of Social Security benefits may be taxable:

| Provisional Income* | Taxable Portion |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| Under $25,000 (single) / $32,000 (MFJ) | 0% |
| $25,000–$34,000 (single) / $32,000–$44,000 (MFJ) | Up to 50% |
| Over $34,000 (single) / $44,000 (MFJ) | Up to 85% |

*Provisional income = AGI + non-taxable interest + 50% of SS benefits

## Self-Employment Tax (2025)

- 15.3% on net SE income up to $176,100 (Social Security portion applies up to this wage base)
- 2.9% Medicare portion applies to all net SE income above $176,100
- 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax on SE income over $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ)
- Deduct: 50% of SE tax as adjustment to income (Schedule 1, line 15)
- Net SE income = Schedule C net profit × 92.35%

## Foreign Income / Accounts

- Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE): $130,000 (2025)
- FBAR (FinCEN 114): Required if foreign accounts exceed $10,000 in aggregate at any time; due April 15 (auto-extension to October 15)
- FATCA (Form 8938): Required if foreign assets exceed $50,000 (single) / $100,000 (MFJ) on last day of year

## Estimated Tax Payments (2025 Q4 / 2026 Schedule)

Pay estimated taxes if you expect to owe $1,000+ after withholding:
- Q4 2025 (Jan 15, 2026)
- Q1 2026 (Apr 15, 2026)
- Q2 2026 (Jun 16, 2026)
- Q3 2026 (Sep 15, 2026)

Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax (110% if prior year AGI > $150,000)

## Penalty & Interest Reference

- Failure to file: 5%/month of unpaid tax (max 25%)
- Failure to pay: 0.5%/month of unpaid tax (max 25%)
- Underpayment of estimated tax: Form 2210; federal short-term rate + 3%
- Accuracy-related penalty: 20% of underpayment

## Common Document Checklist

### Income Documents
- W-2 from each employer
- 1099-NEC (freelance/contractor income ≥ $600)
- 1099-MISC (miscellaneous income)
- 1099-INT (interest income ≥ $10)
- 1099-DIV (dividends)
- 1099-B (brokerage sales — capital gains/losses)
- 1099-R (retirement distributions)
- SSA-1099 (Social Security benefits)
- 1099-G (unemployment compensation, state tax refund)
- K-1 (pass-through income from partnerships/S-corps/trusts)
- 1099-K (payment card/third-party network transactions ≥ $600 in 2025)

### Deduction Documents
- Mortgage interest statement (Form 1098)
- Property tax records
- Charitable donation receipts (written acknowledgment for $250+)
- Medical expense records (insurance premiums, out-of-pocket, mileage)
- Business expense records (receipts, mileage log)
- Student loan interest (Form 1098-E)
- Tuition statements (Form 1098-T)
- Childcare provider info (name, address, EIN/SSN)
- Home office records (square footage, expenses)

### Last Year's Return
- Prior year AGI (needed for e-filing identity verification)
- Carryforward losses, unused credits, basis information

## State Tax Considerations

Every state has different rules. Key questions for state filing:
1. Does your state conform to federal TCJA / Big Beautiful Bill provisions?
2. What is your state's standard deduction and brackets?
3. Does your state tax retirement income (SS, pensions, 401k distributions)?
4. Does your state have reciprocity agreements if you worked in multiple states?
5. Did you change domicile during the year? (Part-year returns required)

States with no income tax (2025): AK, FL, NV, NH, SD, TN, TX, WA, WY
(NH taxes interest & dividends only — phasing out)

## Identity Theft and Security

- IRS IP PIN program: Proactively request a 6-digit Identity Protection PIN at IRS.gov/ippin
- Never email SSN or sensitive tax documents unencrypted
- Shred documents with SSN, EIN, account numbers

---

## Red Flags for IRS Audit

The IRS uses discriminant function scores. Higher risk factors:
- Large charitable deductions relative to income
- Home office deduction (especially large %)
- 100% business use of vehicle
- Schedule C losses, especially repeated
- Claiming refundable credits (EITC, ACTC) — income verification required
- Large gambling income/losses
- Unreported 1099 income (IRS matches these automatically)
- Very high itemized deductions vs. income ratio
