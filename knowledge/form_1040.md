# Form 1040 — U.S. Individual Income Tax Return (Tax Year 2025)

## Overview

Form 1040 is the standard federal income tax return. For tax year 2025 (filed by April 15, 2026,
or October 15 with extension), it consists of the main form plus attachments called Schedules.

---

## Filing Status Options

| Code | Status | Standard Deduction 2025 |
|------|--------|-------------------------|
| 1 | Single | $15,000 |
| 2 | Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) | $30,000 |
| 3 | Married Filing Separately (MFS) | $15,000 |
| 4 | Head of Household (HOH) | $22,500 |
| 5 | Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $30,000 |

Additional standard deduction for age 65+ or blind: $1,600 (single/HOH) or $1,350 (MFJ per person).

---

## Personal Exemption & Dependents

- No personal exemption (suspended by TCJA through 2025+)
- Claim dependents on lines 2a–2c
- Each qualifying child under 17 → Child Tax Credit ($2,000–$2,500 depending on legislation)
- Each other dependent → $500 credit

## Schedule 1 — Additional Income (Line 8 Components)

- Line 1: Taxable refunds of state/local taxes
- Line 2a: Alimony received (pre-2019 agreements only)
- Line 3: Business income/loss → **Schedule C**
- Line 4: Other gains/losses → Form 4797
- Line 5: Rental real estate, royalties, partnerships, S-corps → **Schedule E**
- Line 6: Farm income/loss → Schedule F
- Line 7: Unemployment compensation → 1099-G
- Line 8: Other income (gambling, prizes, etc.)

---

## Adjustments to Income (Schedule 1 Part II — "Above the Line")

These reduce AGI regardless of whether you itemize:

| Line | Deduction | Limit |
|------|-----------|-------|
| 11 | Educator expenses | $300 ($600 MFJ) |
| 12 | Business expenses (reservists/artists) | Form 2106 |
| 13 | Health Savings Account (HSA) | Form 8889; $4,300 self-only / $8,550 family |
| 15 | Student loan interest | $2,500; phased out $75K–$90K (single) |
| 17 | Self-employed SEP/SIMPLE/qualified plans | 25% of net self-employment income |
| 18 | Self-employed health insurance premiums | 100% of premiums |
| 19 | Penalty for early withdrawal of savings | 1099-INT box 2 |
| 20 | Alimony paid (pre-2019 agreements) | Must include recipient SSN |
| 21 | IRA deduction | $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+); income limits apply |
| 22 | Student loan interest | See line 15 |

**Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)** = Total Income − Schedule 1 adjustments (Line 11)

---

## Deductions (Line 12)

Choose the HIGHER of:
- **Standard Deduction** (see table above), OR
- **Itemized Deductions** via **Schedule A**

### Schedule A — Itemized Deductions

| Category | Line | Limit |
|----------|------|-------|
| Medical/dental expenses | 1–4 | Only amount > 7.5% of AGI |
| State/local income or sales taxes (SALT) | 5a/5b | Combined cap: $10,000 (or $30,000 if Big Beautiful Bill enacted) |
| Real estate taxes | 5c | Included in SALT cap |
| Home mortgage interest | 8a | On loans up to $750K |
| Mortgage points | 8c | Spread over loan life or deductible upfront if refinance |
| Charitable cash contributions | 11 | Up to 60% of AGI |
| Charitable non-cash | 12 | Up to 30% of AGI; Form 8283 if >$500 |
| Casualty/theft losses | 15 | Only federally declared disasters; >$100 + 10% AGI floor |

---

## Qualified Business Income Deduction (Line 13)

- 20% (or 23% if Big Beautiful Bill enacted) of qualified business income from pass-through entities
- Applies to sole proprietors, partnerships, S-corps, some trusts
- Subject to W-2 wage and qualified property limits above $191,950 (single) / $383,900 (MFJ)
- Certain "specified service trades or businesses" (SSTBs) phase out above income thresholds

---

## Taxable Income & Tax (Lines 15–16)

```
Line 15: Taxable Income = AGI − Deductions − QBI deduction
Line 16: Tax = Calculated from tax tables or Tax Computation Worksheet
```

## Credits (Lines 19–24)

| Line | Credit | Max Amount |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 19 | Child Tax Credit / Credit for other dependents | $2,000/child under 17; $500 other dep |
| 20 | Schedule 3 Part I credits (education, foreign tax, child & dependent care) | Varies |

| 23 | Excess advance premium tax credit repayment | Form 8962 |
| 24 | **Total Tax** = Line 17 + 22 + 23 − credits | |

### Key Credits Detail

**Child Tax Credit (CTC):**
- $2,000 per qualifying child under 17 (or $2,500 if Big Beautiful Bill enacted)
- Refundable portion (Additional CTC): up to $1,700 per child
- Phase-out: $200,000 (single) / $400,000 (MFJ)

**Child & Dependent Care Credit:**
- 20%–35% of $3,000 (1 dependent) or $6,000 (2+) of care expenses
- Qualifying expense: daycare, after-school, day camp

**Education Credits:**
- American Opportunity Credit: $2,500 (40% refundable); first 4 years of college; income limit $80K–$90K single
- Lifetime Learning Credit: $2,000; no limit on years; income limit $80K–$90K single

**Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) 2025:**
| Children | Max Credit | Income Limit (Single) |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| 0 | $632 | $18,591 |
| 1 | $4,213 | $49,084 |
| 2 | $6,960 | $55,768 |
| 3+ | $7,830 | $59,899 |

**Retirement Savings Contributions Credit (Saver's Credit):**
- 10%–50% of retirement contributions up to $2,000
- Income limit: $38,500 (single) / $76,500 (MFJ)

---

## Other Taxes (Schedule 2)

- Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) — Form 6251
- Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT): 3.8% on investment income above $200K (single) / $250K (MFJ)
- Additional Medicare Tax: 0.9% on wages/SE income above $200K (single) / $250K (MFJ)
- Self-Employment Tax: 15.3% on net SE income (Schedule SE); deduct half on Schedule 1
- Recapture taxes (depreciation, education credits, etc.)

## Key Schedules Summary

| Schedule | Purpose | When Needed |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Schedule A | Itemized deductions | When itemizing > standard deduction |
| Schedule B | Interest and dividends > $1,500 | When interest/div exceeds threshold |
| Schedule C | Business profit/loss | Self-employed, freelancers |
| Schedule D | Capital gains and losses | Sold investments, real estate |
| Schedule E | Rental, S-corp, partnership income | Landlords, business owners |
| Schedule SE | Self-employment tax | Net SE income > $400 |
| Schedule 1 | Additional income and adjustments | Various additional items |
| Schedule 2 | Additional taxes | AMT, NIIT, SE tax |
| Schedule 3 | Additional credits and payments | Various credits |

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Missing 1099-NEC income (IRS cross-checks these)
2. Forgetting state tax refund if you itemized last year
3. Not deducting the employer-equivalent half of SE tax
4. Missing the QBI deduction for pass-through income
5. Incorrect Social Security taxation calculation
6. Not reporting foreign accounts (FBAR requirement separate from 1040)
7. Missing the Saver's Credit if income qualifies
